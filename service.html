Legal Help

We provide Legal Help to those who qualify. To be enitled to Legal Help, you must either be on a passporting benefit or your income must be below the threshold. If you are employed we are able to assess your income to discover whether you do qualify.

Homelessness / Section 202 Appeals

All local authorities have a duty to take a homeless application and make a decision about what duties they owe that person and their household.

If you feel that the local authority have failed to process your application, have given you a negative decision to your homelessness application or have offered you unsuitable accommodation, our specialist legal team can help.

Disrepair Claims

Landlords have a duty to carry out repairs within a reasonable period from the tenant having reported the disrepair in their rented accommodation.

In addition, landlords must ensure their properties are fit for human habitation. If your landlord is not meeting these standards, our specialist legal team can help.

Property Registration / Property Title Problems

All land and property ownership in England and Wales is subject to a system of registration at the HM Land Registry. Land registration confers benefits and safeguards to property owners and you may take advantage of these by applying voluntarily to have your title registered.

If you are having problems or need further assistance, please contact us at info@pewilsonsolicitors.co.uk or speak to a member of our team on 0208 761 3555.

Wills, Probate & Power of Attorneys



Will Writing (Single & Couple) / Codicils (Applications)

If you want to decide how your estate should be distributed after you have died you should make a Will. Where someone dies without having made a valid Will their assets may not necessarily be distributed in the way they would have wanted or in a tax efficient manner.

You cannot amend your will after it’s been signed and witnessed. The only way you can change a will is by making an official alteration called a codicil.

You must sign a codicil and get it witnessed in the same way as witnessing a will.

General/Lasting Power of Attorney/ Registration

A Lasting Power of Attorney (LPA) is a document to cover potential situations in the future where you may lose, or begin to lose, your mental capacity and become incapable of dealing with your own financial affairs, or health and welfare.

A Lasting Power of Attorney gives you the opportunity to select, whilst you are still able to do so, the people that you want to take care of your affairs for you.

Before a Lasting Power of Attorney can be used it must be registered with the Office of the Public Guardian (OPG).

At P.E.Wilson & Co Solicitors, we take away the burden of completing and registering the LPA and we advise you on all aspects of the document along the way.

Probate (Applications & Administration of Estate)

The Grant of Representation (Probate if there is a Will or Administration if there is no Will) is the legal name for the document which will enable those responsible (Executors if there is a Will or Administrators if there is no Will) to deal with the assets and liabilities of the estate.

Witnessing Signitures / Certifying Documents for use in UK & Abroad

When a document requires certification, for example when someone has to prove his or her identity to a third party such as another solicitor or a government department, he or she can attend an appointment with a solicitor with original copies of supporting documents such as a passport or utility bill. The solicitor will then certify that these supporting documents are genuine and can be relied upon to prove the person's identity.
